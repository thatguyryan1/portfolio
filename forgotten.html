Forgotten

2024 | Game Design & Development | Tools: Photoshop, Adobe Animate

For my senior thesis, completed over the last four months of my senior year, I designed and developed a 2D storytelling platformer with the goal of eventually creating it in Unity. Inspired by Celeste and Hollow Knight, I began by mapping out the world, organizing ideas by location, and iterating on the layout to refine the map. I researched game creation techniques, including scalable tile systems, collision mapping, and parallax backgrounds, to guide both level and background design. The game uses an 8x8 pixel tile system, ensuring compatibility across resolutions.

Character creation was a major focus, with multiple iterations for each design—including the main character, based on a wendigo. The plot centers on collectible “memory shards” that influence character interactions, branching dialogue, and story outcomes. Levels were initially created as collision maps and then filled with textures and sprites, with layered parallax backgrounds to add depth. To showcase the game, I created a short teaser trailer with animations highlighting characters, environments, and mechanics.

Throughout development, multiple ideas, levels, and characters were iterated, refined, or removed, demonstrating my process of prototyping and adaptation. While submitted, the project remains a work in progress, reflecting my ongoing exploration of game design, storytelling, and interactive mechanics.
